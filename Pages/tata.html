Ratan Tata's Profile

Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. He was born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India. Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962 and served in various capacities before becoming the chairman of the group in 1991. During his tenure as chairman, he oversaw the growth and diversification of the Tata Group, which today comprises over 100 companies with operations in more than 100 countries. Under Ratan Tata's leadership, the Tata Group made several significant acquisitions, including Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel. He is also credited with launching the world's cheapest car, the Tata Nano. Apart from his corporate achievements, Ratan Tata is known for his philanthropy and social initiatives. He has established the Tata Trusts, which focus on education, healthcare, and rural development in India. He is also a member of several international organizations, including the board of directors of the Ford Foundation and the International Olympic Committee's Ethics Commission. In recognition of his contributions to business and philanthropy, Ratan Tata has received numerous awards and honors, including the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan, two of India's highest civilian honors.
